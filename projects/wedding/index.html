Lakes Region

New Hampshire in the summer is just the best.

We’ve spent the last five years coming to New Hampshire in all seasons - skiing at Loon, hiking in the White Mountains, swimming in Lake Winnipesaukee. We’ve shared so many memories in this area and to us, it’s the best place in the whole universe. Anyone who comes to this special place by definition becomes like family. We hope you get to enjoy the blending of our families as we celebrate together in this spectacular place!

Weather Report

The Lakes Region is gorgeous in August. It's hot during the day, which is perfect for swimming and enjoying the scenery - expect everything to be in full bloom and highs in the 80’s. There’s always a chance of rain up here, but keep your fingers crossed anyway.

At night the temperature drops to the mid 60's, even high 50's. Don't forget to pack a sweater for the cool evenings.

Lodging

Geneva Point Center

Not only are all the events at Geneva Point, but so are we. Between the cabins, the walking paths, the beaches, the views of the mountains, and the ice cream shop, it's quite a peaceful and relaxing place to be. We really hope you'll stay with us.

Reserve a Cabin Here by Feb 29th

We have enough space on site for everybody, but the cabins are rustic and cover a range of lodging options. Some have double beds and private bathrooms and air conditioning, and others just have bunk beds. And there’s everything in between. Most of the cabins sleep 4 people and have an indoor bathroom. Cabins range in price from $120 - $160 per night, or you can camp for a very small fee.

Here's an album with more photos of the cabins.

There aren’t enough cabins for us to give couples their own place. So, if you want to stay on site, expect to share with others. Of course, we will pair you with people you know and / or with whom you request to stay.

We recommend that families stay on site because the kids will love it and it’ll be easy to put them to bed before coming back to the dance floor. If you want something more upscale, we recommend staying offsite. Feel free to reach out to us with any questions.

We’re asking people who want to stay on site to fill out the form by February 29th. Once we have a head count, we’ll reach out to you and give you a cabin assignment. From there, you’ll need to call Geneva Point directly and make your reservation.
